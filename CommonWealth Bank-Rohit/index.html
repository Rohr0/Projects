Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

1.Be careful what you click on.

Don't open links in emails or download stuff from unknown sources. Instead, go to your bank's website or app to check for updates. Clicking on shady links can put harmful software on your device and expose your personal info.

2.Use strong passwords.

Don't share your passwords, and change them regularly. It's a good idea to use a password manager app to keep track of them.

3.Turn on extra security.

Use something like two-step verification. It means you need both a password and another code to get into your accounts.

4.Keep your device safe.

Use antivirus software and keep your computer and phone up to date. If you store info in the cloud, check if it's secure and understand the costs.

5.Use your own device.

Avoid public computers for your bank stuff. They might have sneaky software. If you must use one, clear your history and use strong passwords.

6.Be careful where you browse.

Make sure you see "https" in the website address and a locked padlock icon. Don't visit other sites while you're logged into your bank. Always log out when you're done.

7.Watch out on public Wi-Fi.

Some networks in public places aren't safe. Wait until you're on a trusted, secure network to access your bank accounts. At home, use strong Wi-Fi encryption and passwords.

8.Check your bank statements.

Look at your account activity and statements as soon as you can. Make sure your bank has your current contact info. If you see a mistake or something strange, tell your bank right away.
